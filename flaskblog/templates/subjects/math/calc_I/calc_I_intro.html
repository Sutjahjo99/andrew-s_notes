    Welcome to Calculus I - Introduction

    Calculus is the mathematical study of change in a system, does not sound too difficult right? Let's take a look at a few real life examples of "change in a system":

    • Pouring water into your cup. (The water level is increasing, that is a change!)
    • Accelerating the car by stepping on the gas pedal. (The velocity of the car is increasing)
    • Turning on the heater in your room to heat it up. (The temperature in the room is changing)
    Although all of these changes may be represented with different mathematical formulas, they are still changes that can be studied analytically! You can find out how many degrees your room is heating up by the minute, how fast the car is increasing in speed, etc. Isn't that interesting...?
