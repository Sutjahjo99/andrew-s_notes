Chapter 1: Mole Balance


1.2 General Mole Balance Equation

Before performing any sort of mole balance on a system, it is important for the system to first be defined. The tool that helps determine what a system is, is called a system boundary. The volume enclosed in the system boundary would be the system volume. Take a look at the image below:

Where:

  • \(F_{j_o}\) is the flow rate of material \(j\) going into the system,
  • \(F_{j}\) is the flow rate of material \(j\) going out of the system,
  • \(G_{j}\) is the rate of material \(j\) being generated in the system.
With all of that information, applying a general mole balance on the system volume would look like this:

\( F_{j_o} - F_{j} + G_{j} = \frac{dN_j}{dt} \)

or

\( In - Out + Generation = Accumulation\)

Where \(\frac{dN_j}{dt}\) is the accumulation rate of material \(j\) in the system. If all of the system variables are spatially uniform throughout, we may present the generation rate, \(G_j\), as follows:

\( G_j = r_j \cdot V_{system}\)

Where \(r_j\) is the rate of formation of \(j\) and \(V_{system}\) is the volume of the system. Suppose that the rate of formation, \(r_j\), varies at different positions in the system as such:

Where:

  • \(r_{j_1}\) is the rate of formation of material \(j\) at position 1,
  • \(r_{j_2}\) is the rate of formation of material \(j\) at position 2,
  • \(\Delta V_1\) is the sub-volume which corresponds to \(r_{j_1}\),
  • \(\Delta V_2\) is the sub-volume which corresponds to \(r_{j_2}\).
Then we can define the rate of generation for positions 1 and 2 as:

\( \Delta G_{j_1} = r_{j_1} \cdot \Delta V_1 \)

\( \Delta G_{j_2} = r_{j_2} \cdot \Delta V_2 \)

If the system volume is divided into n number of sub-volumes, we can then re-write the system generation rate, \(G_j\), as:

\[ G_{j} = \sum_{i=1}^{n} \Delta G_{ji} = \sum_{i=1}^{n} r_{ji} \cdot \Delta V_i \]

Taking things a step further, we can take the appropriate limits:

\[ G_j = \lim_{(\Delta V, n)\to(0, \infty)} \sum_{i=1}^{n} r_{ji} \cdot \Delta V_i = \int^V r_j \cdot dV \]

We can now substitute the new generation rate into our general mole balance equation to obtain our new mole balance equation:

\( F_{j_o} - F_{j} + G_{j} = \frac{dN_j}{dt} \)

\( \downarrow \)

\( F_{j_o} - F_{j} + \int^V r_j \cdot dV = \frac{dN_j}{dt} \)

From here, we can develop design equations for industrial reactors such as:

  • Batch reactors
  • Semi-batch reactors
  • Continuous reactors
With the design equations, we will be able to determine the time (for batchr reactors) and the reactor volume (for continuous-flow) necessary to convert a specified amount of reactants into products.

Quick Notes

Equations Used
  • \( F_{j_o} - F_{j} + G_{j} = \frac{dN_j}{dt} \)
  • \( G_j = r_j \cdot V_{system} \)
  • \( G_{j} = \int^V r_j \cdot dV \)

Standard Units
  • \( F_{j_o}, F_{j}, G_{j}, \frac{dN_j}{dt} = \frac{moles}{time} \)
  • \( r_j = \frac{moles}{volume \cdot time} \)
  • \( V = volume \)
