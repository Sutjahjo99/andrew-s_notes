Chapter 1: Mole Balance


1.3 Batch Reactors

General Information

Batch reactors are 1 out of the 4 main reactors types used in industry. They are used for small-scale operations and there are a few reasons why a batch reactor is chosen over a full-scale operation:

  • A new process has just been developed
  • The product to be manufactured is expensive
  • The process is too difficult to convert into a continuous process
Here are some advantages to using a batch reactor:
  • High product conversions by leaving the reactants in the reactor for extended periods of time
As well as disadvantages using it:
  • High labor cost per batch for the operators needed to control and overlook the process



Design Equation of a Batch Reactor

Let's discuss about the mole balance in batch reactors. A batch reactor does not have an inflow nor an outflow, which can be written as (\(F_{j_o} = F_j = 0\)). Hence, the resulting mole balance is:

\[ G_j = \frac{dN_j}{dt} \longrightarrow \frac{dN_j}{dt} = \int^V r_j \cdot dV \]

If the reaction mixture is perfectly mixed such that there are no variation in rate of reaction throughout the reactor volume, we can integrate as follows:

\[ \frac{dN_j}{dt} = r_j \cdot \int^V dV = r_j \cdot V\]

If we take a simple example:

\(\ce{A -> B}\)

As the reaction proceed, the number of moles of A will decrease and the number of moles of B will increase. The mole vs. time trajectory will look as such:

Where If we are trying to find out the number of moles of A at time \(t_1\), we can rearrange the mole balance equation for batch reactors to get the follow:

\[ \frac{dN_A}{dt} = r_A \cdot V \longrightarrow dt = \frac{dN_A}{r_A \cdot V}\]

Doing a simple integration with limits from \(t_0\) and \(t_1\), we can see that:

\[ t_1 - t_0 = \int_{N_{A_o}}^{N_{A_1}}{\frac{dN_A}{r_A \cdot V}} = \int_{N_{A_1}}^{N_{A_0}}{\frac{dN_A}{-r_A \cdot V}}\]

This is a the integral form of the mole balance on a batch reactor. This equation can be used to calculate how much time is needed needed to reduce the number moles of A from time \(t_0\) to \(t_1\).

Quick Notes

Equations Used
  • \( F_{j_o} - F_{j} + G_{j} = \frac{dN_j}{dt} \)
  • \( G_j = r_j \cdot V_{system} \)
  • \( G_{j} = \int^V r_j \cdot dV \)

Standard Units
  • \( F_{j_o}, F_{j}, G_{j}, \frac{dN_j}{dt} = \frac{moles}{time} \)
  • \( r_j = \frac{moles}{volume \cdot time} \)
  • \( V = volume \)
