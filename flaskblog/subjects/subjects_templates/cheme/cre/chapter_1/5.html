Chapter 1: Mole Balance


1.4.1 Continuously-Stirred Tank Reactors (CSTR)

General Information

Commonly known as vats and backmix reactors, CSTRs are primarily used for liquid phase reactions. Since it is a continuous reactor, the calculations performed under the well-mixed model which states that the reactor is operated at steady state and is assumed to be perfectly mixed. This means that the temperature and concentration of the liquid is the same at the exit as they are everywhere else. It is when mixing is highly non-ideal when the well-mixed model is inadequate and will require other modeling techniques such as the residence time distribution to obtain useful results.



Design Equation of a CSTR

Let's discuss about the mole balance in CSTRs. A CSTR is assumed to be operating at steady-state, which can be written as (\(\frac{dN_j}{dt} = 0\)). Hence, the resulting mole balance is:

\[ F_{j_o} - F_{j} + \int^V r_j \cdot dV = \frac{dN_j}{dt} \]

\( \downarrow \)

\[ F_{j_o} - F_{j} + \int^V r_j \cdot dV = 0 \]

Also, because of the well-mixed model we are using, there are no spatial variations in the reactor, which means that:

\[ \int^V r_j \cdot dV = r_j \cdot V \]

Which will turn out mole balance equation into:

\[ F_{j_o} - F_{j} + \int^V r_j \cdot dV = 0 \]

\( \downarrow \)

\[ F_{j_o} - F_{j} + r_j \cdot V = 0 \]

\( \downarrow \)

\[ V = \frac{F_{j} - F_{j_o}}{r_j} \]

\( \downarrow \)

\[ V = \frac{F_{j_o} - F_{j}}{-r_j} \]

The design equation of a CSTR gives us the reactor volume, V, necessary to reduce the entering flow rate of species j from the \(F_{j_o}\) to \(F_j\), when species j is disappearing at a rate of \(-r_j\). To take a step further, we can represent the flow rates \(F_{j_o}\) and \(F_j\) as:

\( F_j = C_j \cdot \upsilon \)

Which shows that the flow rate is equals to the concentration and velocity of the material, making out final design equation to be:

\[ V = \frac{C_{j_o} {\upsilon}_o - C_j {\upsilon}}{-r_j} \]

Quick Notes

Equations Used
  • \( F_{j_o} - F_{j} + \int^V r_j \cdot dV = \frac{dN_j}{dt} \)
  • \( G_{j} = \int^V r_j \cdot dV \)
  • \( F_j = C_j \cdot \upsilon \)
  • \( V = \frac{C_{j_o} {\upsilon}_o - C_j {\upsilon}}{-r_j} \)

Standard Units
  • \( F_{j_o}, F_{j}, G_{j}, \frac{dN_j}{dt} = \frac{moles}{time} \)
  • \( r_j = \frac{moles}{volume \cdot time} \)
  • \( \upsilon = \frac{volume}{time} \)
  • \( C_j = \frac{moles}{volume} \)
  • \( V = volume \)
